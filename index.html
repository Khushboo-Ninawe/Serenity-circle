How are mental disorders diagnosed ?


Mental disorders are health conditions involving changes in thinking, emotion or behavior (or a combination of these). Mental disorders can be associated with distress and/or problems functioning in social, work or family activities.
  • Psychological testing: This can involve a variety of tests, such as personality tests, intelligence tests, and neuropsychological tests. These tests can help the professional to better understand the person's mental state.
  • Physical examination: This can rule out any medical conditions that may be causing the person's symptoms.
  • Personality disorders: Personality disorders are long-term patterns of behavior and inner experiences that differ significantly from what is expected.


What are the long-term effects of mental disorders?


The long-term effects of mental disorders can vary depending on the type of disorder, the severity of the symptoms, and the individual's response to treatment:
  • Social isolation: People with mental disorders may withdraw from social activities and relationships. This can lead to loneliness, isolation, and feelings of worthlessness.
  • Reduced productivity: Mental disorders can interfere with a person's ability to work or go to school. This can lead to financial problems and difficulty supporting themselves and their families.
  • Substance abuse: People with mental disorders are more likely to abuse alcohol and drugs. This can worsen the symptoms of the mental disorder and lead to additional problems, such as legal trouble and health problems.
  • Use of alcohol or recreational drugs
